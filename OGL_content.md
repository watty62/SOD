## Updated August 2020

The table below shows the current permissions to reuse the content of Scottish Local Authorities' websites. Many are lacking in clarity, have messy wording, are vague or misunderstand terminologies. 

The Scottish Government's [own site](https://gov.scot) is excellent and clea: [permitting](https://www.gov.scot/crown-copyright/) all content except logos to be be resused under the [Open Government Licence](http://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/doc/open-government-licence/). There is no good reason why all of the public sector, including local government, should not adopt the terms of OGL. 

At present only Falkirk council allows, and promotes OGL re-use. 

Since 2015 the public sector has been obliged by the [Re-use of Public Sector Information (RPSI) Regulations](https://www.legislation.gov.uk/uksi/2015/1415/contents/made) to permit reuse of information held by local authorities. So, even if Scottish LAs have not yet adopted OGL for all content, they should make it clear how a citizen can re-use their data and information from their website. In my latest trawsl through the T&Cs and Copyright Statements of 32 Scottish Local Authorities, I found only x refrencing RPSI rights there. See the expanded table below.
